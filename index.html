🚀 Quick Start Guide

Get your FIRE projection in just a few steps.

  1. 1 Set your starting assets — enter the total savings or investments you have today in Initial Setup below.
  2. 2 Add historical data — enter at least 1–3 years of your annual income, spending, and investment return rate.
  3. 3 Choose your models — keep the defaults or pick an alternative salary / cost / return model in Fit Options.
  4. 4 Click "Auto-Fit & Predict" — the simulator fits the models to your data and projects your FIRE timeline and charts.
New here? Try Use Sample Data to see instant output.

⚙️ Initial Setup

📋 Historical Data

Year The calendar year of this historical record. Salary ($) Your total gross annual income (salary, freelance, etc.) for this year. Costs ($) Your total annual living expenses and spending for this year. Return Rate (%) The actual investment return rate achieved this year. Enter 8 for 8%. Used to fit the return model. Extra Asset Δ ($) One-time asset changes not captured by income/costs (e.g. inheritance, property sale). Use a negative value for outflows like a home purchase.

🧮 Fit Options

Leave at 0 to auto-estimate from your data shape (recommended). Or set manually: enter how many years you had already worked before your first recorded year. For example, set 5 if your first data year was your 6th career year (places you at x = 6 on the experience axis). The estimated value is shown here after each fit.

% per year The constant annual investment return to use for all future years. A typical long-run stock-market nominal return is around 7–8%.

Runs the models with the options above, then shows parameter panels below where you can fine-tune.
